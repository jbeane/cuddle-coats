Cuddle Coats uses donated furs to offer comfort, warmth, and rehabilitation to injured and orphaned animals before their release into the wild. That's right, fur that was once brutally taken from animals can now be used as a source of care and compassion.

Learn How It Works

Prep session
We take something tragic and turn it into an opportunity for healing by giving furs, widely acknowledged to be cruel and out of fashion, back to animals who need them. Orphaned and injured animals gain comfort and solace in the furs while they are in captivity, and the furs help to minimize contact with humans, helping to improve their chances of survival when they are released back into the wild. Learn about the process.

Why We Need Your Help

Our biggest challenge is raising funds to help pay for the shipping of the Cuddle Coats. Furs are heavy, and even though we painstakingly remove all of the linings, batting, buttons, and clasps, the pelts themselves still result in heavy boxes that are expensive to ship. You contributions will help us get Cuddle Coats to baby animals who need them to not only survive, but thrive in their rehab process. Donate Today!

Our Partners

Boxes ready to be shipped to our partners
We provide furs at no cost to over fifty wildlife rehabilitation clinics and independent rehabbers across the United States and have taken in over 800 fur garments since our inception. We have heard time and again from donors that they are so relieved to have found a positive way to pass on their fur items and from rehabbers who have seen the furs able to comfort babies who were not soothed by terrycloth towels.
